How Google Maps Actually Works

Google Maps ranking is driven by 3 core factors:

1. Relevance

How well the business matches the search query.

2. Distance

How close the business is to the user.

3. Prominence (Most Important)

This is where reviews matter. Prominence includes: rating, number of reviews, review velocity, keywords in reviews, and overall engagement.

User Behavior

When someone searches "barber near me" or "hair salon" they typically:

  1. Look at top 3 results
  2. Compare rating and number of reviews
  3. Click 1–2 options max
You don't need to be #1 — but you must be competitive.

Core Metrics

Rating (Stars)

First filter for trust. Anything below ~4.4 starts losing clicks.

RatingPerception
4.8–5.0Premium / top choice
4.5–4.7Strong
4.2–4.4Risky
<4.2Avoided

Review Count

Social proof and confidence signal. 4.6 with 300 reviews beats 4.6 with 50 reviews almost every time.

Review Velocity

How often new reviews are added. Signals the business is active and keeps ranking fresh. Old or inconsistent reviews cause ranking to drop over time.

Observation Labels

🔴 "costing bookings"

Condition: Rating ≤ 4.3
Customers see better-rated competitors nearby and skip this business during comparison. A few bad reviews dragging the average down with no system to recover unhappy clients.

🟡 "room to improve rating"

Condition: High reviews (120+) but rating < 4.7
Strong base but not optimised. Not asking happy customers for reviews. Negative reviews not managed.

🔵 "low visibility"

Condition: Reviews ≤ 80 and rating ≥ 4.4
Good quality, low volume. No review generation system. Not appearing in top results.

🟣 "high volume, not optimised"

Condition: Reviews ≥ 200 and rating ≤ 4.4
High traffic but poor consistency. Scaling without quality control. Massive lost potential.

Profile Maturity Badges

Shown in the snapshot after analysis. Tells you how long a business has been building their Google presence and whether momentum is still active.

Why Your Messaging Works

You are not saying "we help you get more reviews".

You are saying: "this is what's costing you bookings"

This aligns with how customers behave and how Google ranks businesses.

Mental Model

Google Maps = Competition. Every search is: "Who looks best right now?"

  • Looks worse → loses
  • Looks equal → ignored
  • Looks better → wins

Final Takeaway

You are not selling a service. You are pointing out where they are losing customers right now. That's why this works.
